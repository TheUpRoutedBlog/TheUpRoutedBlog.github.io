OUR HIGHLIGHTS


Alex:        Blah.
Brianna:  Blah.
Cheddar: Blah.


I really hope all Sunday nights in Georgia escalate similarly to the ones in My Moon Hostel, Zugdidi. A simple invitation for khachepuri turned into a huge feast where family and foreigners alike came together to eat a feast of aubergine salads, tripe and selection of cheesey delights, all washed down with homebrewed wine and copius amounts of the deadly 'cha cha': looks like vokda, smells like rum, tastes like a hangover. An elderly glamourous ex-scientist danced the night away with anyone she could get her hands on to anything thing from Georgian folk music to Radiohead, only stopping to give her opinion on everybody's love lives. A couple of touring musicians chased their sun around who had affectionately been named 'mowgli' by the Russian speaking people due to his mop of hair. Needless to say the Khachapuri was divine and the cake that was served alongside it (?) also.

Needless to say we were reluctant to leave the next day, but we had an important appointmant with our visa guy back in Batumi. ALthough when we arrived it seemed like he had had a heavy Sunday night too: the consulate was completely deserted. At 3:30pm on a Monday. As our appointment was at 4, Alex waited for about an hour before asking the local shop keepers if they knew what was going on. Some Azeri car washers were his mates and rang him up. He said he'd be two minutes. An hour of looking through family photo albums later, Alex asked if he could call the guy again. It was getting dangerously close to our train for Tblisi leaving and the banks had already closed; so even if the visas did somehow materialise in the next 20 minutes we had no way of paying for them as you have to pay at a bank.
Back to Batumi Tblisi
